**2. `FmapiChatTransport` satisfies `ChatTransport` (build payload + passthrough).** PASS.
- `send` signature is byte-identical to the protocol (`inspect.signature` parity: `messages`, kw-only `model`, `max_tokens`, `temperature`, `response_format=None`). `isinstance(t, ChatTransport)` is `True` at runtime (`runtime_checkable`).

**3. `WarehouseSqlRunner` satisfies `SqlRunner` (rows→dicts).** PASS.
- `SqlRunner = Callable[[str], list[dict]]` (context.py:62). `WarehouseSqlRunner.__call__(self, sql)` matches; instance is callable and returns row dicts.
- `__call__` normalizes each row to a plain `dict` and treats `None`/empty as `[]` (providers.py:72-73). Both covered (`test_sql_runner_maps_rows_to_dicts`, `test_sql_runner_handles_empty_and_none`).

**4. Errors propagate (not swallowed).** PASS. Neither adapter has a `try/except`; both let exceptions raise. `test_chat_transport_propagates_errors` and `test_sql_runner_propagates_errors` assert `pytest.raises(RuntimeError)`. This matches the oracle's division of labor: `LLMClient.complete` owns retries/`LLMError` (llm.py:182-199) and `ContextGatherer.gather` owns degrade-on-failure (context.py:269-273); the adapters must stay transparent, and they do.

So the SDK is genuinely reachable at call-time and genuinely absent at import-time.
